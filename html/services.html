Tree Plantation

Vidhaigal NGO has undertaken a significant initiative to green the landscape across Kanchipuram, Chengalpattu, Thiruvallur, and Chennai districts. Through dedicated efforts, they have planted over 2.5 lakh trees & 4 Lakh Palm seeds, creating numerous urban forests and micro-forests that contribute significantly to environmental conservation and community well-being.

  4. Ullavur Government School - mini-forest with 1,000 trees.
  5. Walajabad Church - mini-forest with 500 trees.
  6. Walajabad Veterinary Hospital - mini-forest with 1,000 trees.
  7. Maraimalai Nagar - A mini-forest with 1,000 trees.
  8. Uthiramerur - Two mini-forests, one with 2,000 trees on a 4-acre plot, and another with 1,000 trees on an agricultural land.
  9. Arignar Anna Government Boys Higher Secondary School - mini-forest with 1,000 trees.

Plastic Awareness Campaign

Vidhaigal NGO has been conducting a continuous awareness campaign against plastics and promoting the use of cloth bags.

  • Walajabad Bus Stand: A thousand yellow cloth bags were distributed to the public at the Walajabad bus stand, emphasizing the need for cloth bags and creating awareness.
  • Oragadam: A rally was held to highlight the harmful effects of plastics, and over a thousand yellow cloth bags were distributed in Oragadam, an industrial area.
  • Triveni Academy: A yellow cloth bag awareness campaign was conducted among students at Triveni Academy.
  • Varanavasi Government Primary School: A yellow cloth bag awareness campaign was conducted at Varanavasi Government Primary School.
  • Agaram Government Primary School: Students were made aware of the harmful effects of plastics at Agaram Government Primary School.
  • Thenneri Government Middle School: An awareness campaign was conducted at Thenneri Government Middle School.
  • Arignar Anna Government Boys Higher Secondary School: A cloth bag awareness campaign was conducted at Arignar Anna Government Boys Higher Secondary School.
  • RCM Middle School: Students were made aware of the benefits of cloth bags at RCM Middle School.
  • St. Joseph's Matriculation Higher Secondary School: A yellow cloth bag awareness campaign was conducted at St. Joseph's Matriculation Higher Secondary School.

In essence, the Vidhaigal NGO has been actively promoting the use of cloth bags and educating the public about the detrimental effects of plastics through various campaigns in schools and public places.

Water Body Conservation

Vidhaigal NGO an non-profit organization, has been rejuvenating several defunct water bodies, removing plastic waste, and creating awareness.

  • Kanchipuram Corporation: Removed 2 tons of plastic from the Sarvatheertha Lake within the corporation limits.
  • Salavakkam Union, Kanchipuram District: Over 3 tons of plastic was collected from the Palar riverbank in Thirumukoodal and handed over to the panchayat.
  • Oragadam, Kanchipuram District: The Nalla Thanneer (Good Water) lake in Mathur village was cleaned of plastic and awareness was created.
  • Walajabad Union, Kanchipuram District: A defunct water body in Nallur village was deepened, its banks were raised, and it was made available for public use.
  • Uthukottai: Over 2 tons of plastic was removed from the Ellliamman temple tank.
  • Varanavasi, Kanchipuram District: A small, defunct water body was revived.
  • Nattanallur, Kanchipuram District: Prosopis juliflora trees were removed from the Mel Thangal water body, and the watercourse was repaired.

In summary, the Vidhaigal NGO have been actively involved in restoring water bodies by removing plastic waste, deepening water bodies, and clearing invasive vegetation. These efforts aim to improve water quality, increase groundwater recharge, and provide a sustainable water source for the community.

Education

During the COVID-19 pandemic, when schools were closed, Vidhaigal NGO started free classes for students in rural areas, especially in Nathaanallur. Along with regular studies, they also organized extracurricular activities like wall painting to keep the students engaged and motivated. This initiative has been running successfully for four years now, with over 50 students participating.

Empowering Tribal Communities

Vidhaigal NGO has been actively supporting over 300 people in various villages including Valajabad Kattivakkam, Kidiripettai, Uthukadu, Palai Sivapuram, Nattanalur, Devariyambakkam, Avalur, Venpakkam, Thirumukkudal, and Palaveeri. The Irular tribal community, unfortunately, has been lagging behind in educational progress, leading to many individuals succumbing to various challenges. To address this issue, Vidhaigal NGO has been relentlessly working to bring about a positive change.

  • Awareness Programs: Conducting plays and other activities to highlight the importance of education among the Irular community.
  • Educational Support: Providing notebooks, textbooks, school bags, and food items to students.
  • Re-enrollment Campaigns: Identifying students who have dropped out of school, and persuading them and their parents about the significance of education.

The organization aims to improve the educational status of the Irular tribe and empower them to lead better lives.

Reviving Trees

There has been a concerning trend of uprooting and discarding age-old Banyan and Peepal trees, often over 50 or 100 years old, due to road expansion projects. To counter this practice, the Vidhaigal NGO has initiated a "Reviving Trees" project. Under this initiative, the uprooted trees are carefully rescued and transplanted to suitable locations.

  • A century-old Peepal tree, which was obstructing agricultural activities in Nathanallur, has been successfully transplanted to the Walajabad railway station.
  • A 50-year-old Banyan tree from Pillaiyarkuppam has found a new home in the Arignar Anna Government Boys Higher Secondary School playground in Walajabad.
  • A 50-year-old Peepal tree, which was in the way of a road expansion project in Uthukadu, has been replanted on the banks of the Serkaadu Lake.

Over ten such ancient Banyan and Peepal trees have been rescued and given a new lease of life through this project.

Agricultural Training

As a pilot project, a direct agricultural training program was conducted for students in Nathanallur village. The training included hands-on experience in planting seedlings, plowing, mechanized sowing, preparing insect repellents, and weeding. Students from Avalur Government School, Walajabad Industrial Training School, Triveni Academy, Adhi College National Service Scheme, and Agathiya School, totaling 186 students, participated in this training.

Rooftop Gardening Workshop: A one-day workshop on rooftop gardening was conducted for children in Mudichur Varadarajapuram. The workshop covered topics such as setting up a rooftop garden, its maintenance, and preparing natural pesticides. Over 50 children and students participated in this program.

  • Hands-on agricultural training: Students were involved in various farming activities like planting, plowing, and weeding.
  • Mechanized farming demonstration: Students were shown how to use agricultural machinery for sowing.
  • Natural pesticide preparation: Students learned how to make eco-friendly pesticides.
  • Rooftop gardening workshop: The workshop focused on setting up and maintaining rooftop gardens.

Overall, the training programs aimed to provide students with practical agricultural knowledge and encourage them to adopt sustainable farming practices.
